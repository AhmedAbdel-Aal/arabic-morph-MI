Scientific context
==================

We are building a larger Akeel-style dataset for probing Arabic root-pattern morphology inside LLM representations.

The current small baseline dataset is similar to the setup in Akeel et al.: root-template forms, with base and affixed variants, used to probe whether model hidden states encode morphology. The next step is to scale the dataset from naturally occurring Arabic sentences, while keeping enough linguistic cleanliness that probing results are meaningful.

We already built a sentence-to-candidate pipeline:

  Arabic Billion Words sentences
  -> CAMEL analyses
  -> root/template/affix candidates
  -> token-level and type-level views
  -> manual audit sample

The problem: CAMEL plus mechanical template matching produces many candidates that formally match a template but are not good study items. The clearest failure class is lexical/frozen/common nouns that happen to fit a pattern. Example:

  surface: لترابه
  root: ت.ر.ب
  template: فعال
  base/canonical base: تراب
  pos: noun
  sentence: ... العاشقين لترابه ...

This is a normal lexical noun ("soil/dust") with a possessive/prepositional affix, not the kind of productive derivational morphology item we want to treat as evidence about root-pattern composition. We should not simply drop the whole template فعال, because that would also remove valid morphology items. The goal is to identify risk classes and audit them, not to destroy broad coverage.

Important principle:

  Do NOT solve this by deleting all nouns.
  Do NOT solve this by deleting all rows for templates like فعال / فعيل / فعول.
  Do NOT treat CAMEL as gold truth.
  Do NOT claim the generated data is final gold without audit.

Known examples / issues to keep in mind
=======================================

1. Lexical concrete/common nouns that fit a pattern mechanically:
   - تراب / لترابه as فعال, root ت.ر.ب
   - فريق / الفريق as فعيل, root ف.ر.ق
   - الحكومة as فعول, root ح.ك.م

2. Proper names, places, nationalities, and named entities:
   - لبنان / لبناني
   - محمود
   These may have roots/templates in CAMEL but are often bad controlled morphology items.

3. Context mismatch:
   Example from candidates:
   - "باسم الحكومة"
   CAMEL can analyze باسم as "smiling" from ب.س.م with template فاعل, but in context it means "in the name of / on behalf of", not the morphological target we want.

4. Ambiguous CAMEL analyses:
   Some tokens have many possible analyses. The pipeline currently picks candidate analyses by matching root/template/surface. High ambiguity should be treated as a risk signal, not automatically accepted.

5. Surface spelling rules:
   Example:
   - canonical_base_form: كتابة
   - surface_stem before possessive suffix: كتابت
   This is correct. Do not confuse bound surface stem with canonical base.

Step 2: Design risk flags
-------------------------

Add explicit risk flags to rows instead of deleting rows.

Suggested flags include, but are not limited to:

  risk_lexical_noun_heavy_template
    Noun row in a template known to contain many frozen lexical nouns, such as فعال, فعيل, فعول, فعلان, فعلاء.

  risk_proper_or_place_name_like
    Looks like a proper name, place, nationality, or named entity.
    You may use CAMEL gloss/lex fields, capitalization is not useful in Arabic, sentence context, or simple lexical cues.

  risk_context_mismatch_candidate
    The CAMEL analysis/root/template seems plausible in isolation but mismatches the sentence meaning.
    This may be difficult to automate fully; at least surface it for audit.

  risk_high_camel_ambiguity
    CAMEL returned many analyses. Pick a threshold after inspecting the distribution.

  risk_functionalized_or_idiomatic_use
    Example: باسم in باسم الحكومة.

  risk_template_pos_mismatch
    Template/POS combination is suspicious for this study.

  risk_normalized_surface_only
    Match depends on normalization or surface rewrite in a way that deserves audit.

  safe_derived_verb
    Verb rows in templates such as استفعل / انفعل, when surface and root match cleanly.

  safe_participle_or_adjective
    Clear adjective/participle-like rows, especially فاعل, مفعول, مفتعل where context agrees.

  safe_verbal_noun_candidate
    Clear verbal noun/masdar-like rows, e.g. افتعال / فعالة when context agrees.

You can revise names, but keep them simple and documented.

Every row should get:

  risk_flags: list[str]
  risk_bucket: one of low / medium / high
  risk_notes: short machine-readable explanation if helpful
